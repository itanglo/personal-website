About Me

My name is Paolo Di Pasquale, I'm a web developer from Italy 🇮🇹, living in England 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿. I was born in a little coastal town in Sicily in the mid 80s 🕺. We moved to England in the early 90s 📀, to a small village next to the river Thames in Oxfordshire.

As a kid, I remember playing on a Commodore 64, especially during the long afternoons, while I waited for my parents to wake up and take me to the beach. I graduated to the Amiga 500 💾, which I mainly still used for playing games 🕹️, but I also discovered Deluxe Paint 2 and spent countless hours reproducing my favourite football players in pixel form.

After finishing my A-Levels 📚, I took the plunge into the world of work 🔨and tried my hand at a few different things, whilst working out what I really wanted to do. During this time, I had a chat with a team mate from the local football ⚽ team and he offered me an opportunity to work at the IT recruitment company he was managing in London. The chance to work in London, earn money 💶 and get exposure to the IT industry was really appealing to me, so I decided to go for it.

I started developing a real interest in the web and making websites so I started learning from books in my spare time. It was just a hobby on the side for a while, but around the time my parents were getting divorced, I really started to dedicate as much time as I could to learning about web development. It was probably a form of escapism, but it was the first time that I thought this could eventually lead to a career.

I moved temporarily back to Sicily with my dad and very briefly thought about a future there. Unfortunately I soon realised that opportunities for work 💼 were few and far between so I decided to come back to England. As serendipity would have it, I met my wife ❤️, also from Sicily, shortly after that and it felt like my life was really starting to get going.

I had another brief spell in recruitment, before working in a few other sales roles and I actually did quite well and got a few promotions along the way. Web development was always there in the background and in my spare time I would be tinkering on websites and writing in my blog.

Eventually I got a break via a family friend who worked on the Customer Services team at Perform Group (now DAZN). I had frequently posted web development stuff and links to my blog on Facebook and one day my friend sent me a message one day saying they were hiring for someone to join the Web Support team.

I was very inexperienced and nervous and I recall my leg had seized up on the way to the interview. It was a good job my wife and daughter had come with me, because halfway down the M4 I had to pull over so she could drive 🚗 (my wife that is, my daughter had only just had her first birthday). I hobbled into the meeting room and after a brief chat and a technical test (on paper) I got the job 🤝. In my usual style, I explained I knew there were many gaps in my knowledge, to which the hiring manager replied "sometimes it's not what you know, but who you know."

The rest, as they say, is history and you can read about it on my CV 📜.
