About Me

I'm Paolo Di Pasquale, a web developer from Italy 🇮🇹, living in England 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿. I was born in a little coastal town in Sicily in the 80s and we moved to England in the early 90s to a small village near the River Thames in Oxfordshire.

As a kid, I remember playing on a Commodore 64, especially during the long afternoons, while I waited for my parents to wake up and take me to the beach. I graduated to the Amiga 500 💾, which I still mainly used for playing games 🕹️, but I also discovered Deluxe Paint 2 and spent countless hours reproducing my favourite football players in pixel form.

After completing my A-Levels, I took the plunge into the world of work and tried my hand at a few different things, whilst working out what I really wanted to do. During this time, I was offered an opportunity to work at an IT recruitment company in London.

Speaking to candidates and clients, I developed a real interest in the web and making websites. I started learning from books and any online resources I could find. It started as a hobby, but around the time my parents were getting divorced, I began dedicating much more time to it. That’s when I realised it could become a career.

I moved back to Sicily with my dad and briefly considered a future there, but opportunities for work were few and far between, so I decided to come back to England.

I had a brief spell at another IT recruitment company before moving on to work in other sales roles and getting several promotions in the process.

My personal life started to take off too, and I met my wife ❤️ (also from Sicily) and started a family. That gave me the drive to pursue a career in web development. Eventually, I got a break through a friend at Perform Group (now DAZN) to join the Web Support team.

I was inexperienced and very nervous. My leg had seized up on the way to the interview, and halfway down the M4 I had to pull over so my wife could drive the rest of the way. I hobbled into the meeting room. After a brief chat and a technical test, I got the job 🤝. It was the opportunity I’d been working towards for years, and the point where things really started to come together. You can read more about what happened next on my CV.
